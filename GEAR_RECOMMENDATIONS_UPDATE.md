### 3️⃣ **Computer Requirements for Editing (NEW SECTION)**

#### **Mac (Highly Recommended)**

**Best editing experience on a Mac with Apple M1-M5 chips** (blazing-fast performance + energy efficiency)

##### **💻 Minimum Specs (Starting Students)**
- **16GB RAM** — Minimum for smooth Lightroom performance
- **256GB SSD** — Enough for OS + apps + moderate photo library
- **M1 chip or newer** — Fast, efficient, future-proof
- **Budget**: $800-1,200 (MacBook Air M1/M2)

##### **🚀 Ideal Specs (Growing Photographers)**
- **32GB RAM** — Ideal for multitasking, large photo libraries, and advanced editing
- **512GB-1TB SSD** — Room for growing photo library + video projects
- **M2/M3 chip** — Faster rendering, smoother multitasking
- **Budget**: $1,500-2,500 (MacBook Pro M2/M3)

##### **⚡ Professional Setup (What Italo Uses)**
- **MacBook Pro 15" M1**
- **64GB RAM** — Runs multiple apps simultaneously with zero lag
- **1TB SSD** — Fast storage for large photo/video libraries
- **Quote**: _"I edit with speed and have multiple applications open at once. This is professional-level, not required for starting students—but shows what's possible."_ — Italo
- **Budget**: $3,000-4,500 (MacBook Pro M1 Pro/Max/Ultra with 64GB RAM)

#### **PC/Windows (Alternative)**
- **Status**: PCs work fine for Lightroom
- **Mac Advantages**: Better color accuracy, battery life, and creative workflow optimization
- **Minimum Specs**: 16GB RAM, Intel i5/AMD Ryzen 5 or better, 256GB SSD
- **Budget**: $700-1,200
