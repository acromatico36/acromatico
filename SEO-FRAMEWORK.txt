Part 1: Content Structure (The Actual Post)
TITLE TAG (META TITLE)
Character Limit: 55-60 characters (including spaces)
Formula:
Examples:
"7 Best Photography Classes for Kids (2026 Guide)"
"Powel Crosley Wedding Photography | Sarah & Mike"
"How Kids Ages 10-14 Can Earn $1,500+ With Photography"
Rules:
Primary keyword must appear in title
Keep under 60 characters or Google truncates
Include year for freshness signal
Make it click-worthy but not clickbait
META DESCRIPTION
Character Limit: 150-160 characters (including spaces)
Formula:
Examples:
"Compare the top 7 online photography courses for kids ages 7-14. Pricing, earning potential, and which programs teach business skills. Updated 2026."
"Stunning waterfront wedding at Powel Crosley Estate in Sarasota. View photos, venue details, and timeline from Sarah & Mike's spring celebration."
Rules:
Summarize the post's value
Include primary keyword naturally
End with action word (view, discover, learn, compare)
Don't duplicate title tag
URL SLUG
Formula:
Examples:
Rules:
Lowercase only
Hyphens between words (not underscores)
3-6 words maximum
Include primary keyword
NO dates in URL (makes content look old)
NO /2026/01/25/ date structure
H1 HEADING (MAIN TITLE ON PAGE)
Can differ from Title Tag Formula:
Examples:
"The Only Photography Education Your Child Needs to Start Earning"
"Powel Crosley Estate Wedding Photography | Sarah & Mike's Waterfront Celebration"
"From Hobby to Income: How Kids Build Photography Businesses"
Rules:
Only ONE H1 per page
60-70 characters ideal
More emotional/engaging than title tag
Primary keyword must appear
Make it compelling for humans (title tag is for search engines)
OPENING SECTION (FIRST 300 WORDS)
This is CRITICAL. Most readers (and AI platforms) decide in 10 seconds if they'll stay.
Direct Answer (First 30-50 Words)
Answer the main question immediately. This is what AI platforms cite.
Hook/Problem Statement (30-50 Words)
Identify the problem or create curiosity.
"Most photography courses for children teach basic camera settings and composition. But they miss the bigger opportunity: teaching kids to turn
What They'll Learn (100-150 Words)
Outline the post's value. Use bullet points or numbered list.
Rules:
Answer the question in first 30 words (AI citation hook)
Use "you" language (not "we" or "I" initially)
No fluff or backstory yet
Make every word count
Include primary keyword in first 100 words
TABLE OF CONTENTS
Add this after your opening section for posts over 1,500 words.
Format:
Benefits:
Improves user experience (scannable)
Can trigger sitelinks in Google results
AI platforms parse TOCs to understand structure
Increases time on page (users jump to sections)
H2 SECTIONS (MAIN CONTENT BODY)
Quantity: 5-8 H2 sections per post Length: 400-800 words per H2 section Formula:
Structure Within Each H2 Section:
Opening (50-80 words): One-sentence direct answer, context/why it matters, what they'll learn
Body (300-600 words): 3-5 key points (use H3 subheadings), bullet lists, data/statistics, examples, original insights
Closing (50-100 words): Summary takeaway, transition to next section, internal link to related content
H3 SUBHEADINGS (WITHIN H2 SECTIONS)
Use H3s to break up H2 sections into digestible chunks.
Example:
H2: "How Kids Ages 10-14 Can Actually Earn Money With Photography"
H3: "Entry-Level Gigs (Ages 10-12)"
H3: "Intermediate Gigs (Ages 12-14)"
H3: "Real Student Examples"
Rules:
2-4 H3s per H2 section
Keep H3 sections to 150-250 words each
Use H3s to organize lists, examples, or sub-topics
Never skip heading levels (don't go H2 → H4)
CONTENT FORMATTING RULES
Paragraph Length:
Maximum 80 words per paragraph
Ideal: 40-60 words
Short paragraphs = better readability on mobile
AI platforms prefer shorter paragraphs (easier to parse)
Sentence Length:
Average 15-20 words per sentence
Mix short (5-10 words) and medium (15-25 words)
Avoid sentences over 30 words
Lists:
Use bullet points or numbered lists for 3+ related items, steps in a process, features or benefits, or examples.
Bold Text:
Use for emphasis on key terms (sparingly)
Bold the first phrase of list items
Bold important statistics or numbers
Don't overuse (loses impact)
COMPARISON TABLES (HIGH-VALUE CONTENT)
When to Use: Comparing products/services/platforms, showing pricing tiers, feature comparisons, before/after data
Rules:
Keep to 6-8 columns max (mobile readability)
Bold your brand name
Use checkmarks/X for yes/no columns (or Yes/No text)
Make YOUR offering stand out
Add context below table explaining key differences
SEO Benefit: Tables are HIGHLY citable by AI platforms
